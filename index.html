Answer no 6 : The time period of a compound pendulumn is minimum if the distance of a point of suspension from the centre of gravity is equal to the radius of gyration.
Question no 7 : How is the period of a compound pendulumn determined?
Answer no 7 : The period of a compound pendulumn is determined by the following formula: (T) = 2π(I/mgh)1/2 where T is the period. I is the moment of inertia of the pendulumn about the axis of rotation, m is the mass of pendulumn, g is the acceleration due to gravity and h is the distance from the centre of mass to the axis of rotation.
Question no 8 : How does the lenght of a compound pendulumn affect its period?
Answer no 8 : The period of a compound pendulumn is directly proportional to the square root of its lenght . Increasing the lenght of the pendulumn will result in a longer period while decreasing the lenght will result in a shorter period.
Question no 9 : How does the mass of a compound pendulumn affect its period?
Answer no 9 : The mass of a compound pendulumn does not directly affect its period . The period is primarily influenced by the lenght and distribution of mass.
Question no 10 : What factors can affect the accuracy of a compound pendulumn's period measurement?
Answer no 10 : Factors that can affect the accuracy of a compound pendulumn's period measurement include air resistance, friction at the axis of rotation and external disturbances like vibrations or drafts.
Question no 11 : How does the location of the axis of rotation affect the period of a compound pendulumn?
Answer no 11 : The location of the axis of rotation does not affect the period of compound pendulumn. The period is determined by the lenght and distribution of mass, regardless from where the pendulumn is suspended from.
Question no 12 : What is the effect of damping on a compound pendulumn's motion?
Answer no 12 : Damping refers to the dissipation of energy in a pendulumn system, typiccaly due to friction or air resistance. Damping causes the amplitude of the pendulumn;s oscillation to decrease over time, resulting in a shorter period.
